Unit 9: Object-Oriented Software Architecture

The key takeaway for me was that architecture is a design decision based on scale, security, maintainability and the needs of the organisation, rather than a hierarchy where one approach is always better.

The subject did not feel as unfamiliar as some earlier units. I already work with systems made up of applications, services, endpoints and deployment processes, and so could relate some of the issues described to scenarios I have experienced.

The reading gave terminology and structure to those experiences. I felt most engaged when it challenged the assumption that microservices are automatically an improvement. I can clearly picture the microservices approach being pushed aggressively, whereas a small monolith may be the correct choice, preventing unnecessary complexity from being introduced long before there is any proven need.
