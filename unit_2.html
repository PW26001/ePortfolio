Unit 2: SOLID Principles of Object-Oriented Design

I particularly enjoyed this Unit, as while studying I was asked a question by my 13 year old son on what the course covered. While explaining it to him I came across a real world scenario which could be improved by a SOLID approach, and was in turn able to demonstrate through a sequence of improvements to a code example exactly how this would be implemented.

I was also motivated by the question to provide not only an understanding of each principle, but a version that I could use to pass on an overview of each to people, primarily my son, who would not want to understand it from a coding perspective.

The Cupboard Sorter artefact shows my developing understanding of SOLID principles. The first version intentionally places everything into one class, while the later versions separate responsibilities into clearer parts. This helped me understand that object-oriented design is not just about using classes, but about placing responsibilities where they belong so that the code is easier to understand, extend and maintain.
