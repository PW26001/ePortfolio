Advanced Object-Oriented Design and Programming

This section contains the artefacts, reflections and development evidence for the Advanced Object-Oriented Design and Programming module.

Module Portfolio

This portfolio records my development across the module, beginning with core object-oriented principles and moving through SOLID design, design patterns, concurrency, secure coding, refactoring, software architecture, testing and dependency management.

The pages below present the journey through the units, with each section including reflection and supporting artefacts where appropriate.

Unit 1

Introduction to object-oriented programming concepts and initial Python exercises.

Unit 2

SOLID principles and early practical application through the cupboard sorter exercise.

Units 3–5

Creational, structural and behavioural design patterns, including Factory Method, Decorator, Strategy and Chain of Responsibility.

Unit 6

Concurrency and parallelism, demonstrated through a thread-safe banking application.

Unit 7

Secure coding practice, including password protection, input validation and safer authentication design.

Unit 8

Refactoring and code smells, focusing on maintainability, readability and technical debt.

Unit 9

Object-oriented software architecture, including monolithic, microservices and layered architectural approaches.

Unit 10

Test-Driven Development, Behaviour Driven Development and unit testing for an e-learning platform component.

Current Development

The portfolio now shows the progression from individual object-oriented principles through to broader architectural and testing concerns. Later units have increasingly brought earlier topics together, particularly where secure design, refactoring, testing and dependency management support maintainable software design.
