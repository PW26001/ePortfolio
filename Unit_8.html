Unit 8: Refactoring and Code Smells

At this point it felt like the focus was to reuse the knowledge built from the SOLID principles and design patterns, and take a ‘this feels wrong’ approach to initial code reviews. I could relate this to my professional background, where a junior staff member may have a good instinct for how to solve a problem, but not the foresight to ensure they do not introduce a different issue through their remediation. ‘Code smells’ was new terminology to me, but one that I felt covered exactly what was needed.

Refactoring that code, and the techniques which can help avoid longer-term technical debt, were subjects I took value from.

The consistent redevelopment, or ‘continuous improvement’, which I have seen become a buzzword within the industry, took on a new aspect for me as I appreciated the necessity of this approach as technology evolves.
