Units 3-5: Design Patterns

Units 3–5 focused on design patterns, covering creational, structural and behavioural patterns in turn. I found these units useful because they helped me move from understanding individual SOLID principles to seeing reusable ways those principles can be applied in code.

I appreciated the structure that these modules introduced, and at this point it influenced my working life. I was encouraged by this new knowledge to create reusable PowerShell modules to improve the process the team follow when migrating between deployment systems, taking care to ensure that all stages implemented abstraction to allow for future updates, while using a consistent reusable structure which would allow for expansion when required.

The learning material repeatedly explained that unnecessary patterns could lead to overcomplication of simple programs and I found this to be true, often expanding and then contracting the modules I was creating to only implement the features that were absolutely needed.

The Cupboard Sorter artefact I have included builds on the earlier Unit 2 work. Rather than forcing every pattern into the example, I applied the patterns that I found to be appropriate: Factory Method, Decorator, Strategy and Chain of Responsibility.

This progression developed my understanding to see that design patterns are ways of making software easier to extend, test and maintain.
